Well, that’s more than enough about me. Let’s get to the part where we talk about what Mastodon is doing for you this year :)

## Mastodon.social: A safe landing place for the social web

Our team runs mastodon.social to provide a safe landing place for anyone, especially for newcomers to the social web. It’s also an important example to others of what a Mastodon server looks like when it’s up and running. For those reasons, and because we feel it’s the right thing to do, we want mastodon.social to be accessible and safe for anyone.

We know we can do more to improve in that regard, so today we’re announcing a new resource for mastodon.social users (though we expect people on other servers and beyond will find it useful too): the [Mastodon Help Centre](https://help.joinmastodon.org/). The Help Centre is intended to be a place where anyone can learn how to use the Mastodon software, and find more information about participating on mastodon.social in a healthy, safe, and accessible way.

We have a lot of ideas about what we want to add here. We’re announcing the Help Centre now because we think it’s important to show our work as we go, and we want your help. Throughout 2026 community team-members Zora and Amelia will continue to add guides and tutorials about Mastodon, and we’ll ask for your input along the way: what kinds of resources should we add? Are we on the right track? How can we improve?

Don’t think we’ve forgotten about mastodon.online… We want to do something wildly different with mastodon.online. Not unsafe, obviously, but… wacky. We have some ideas, but we don’t really know what this looks like yet! If you have ideas, let us know.d

## Default server recommendations and server discovery

While we want anyone to feel safe and welcome on mastodon.social, we also know we can do more to help deliver on Mastodon’s decentralised promise. After Twitter’s purchase in late 2022, we made a strategic decision to send new sign-ups directly to mastodon.social. This choice was driven by our desire to reduce cognitive load for new users and ensure a stable onboarding experience during a period of significant platform migration.

Our first experiment is Default Server Recommendations. (If you were at FOSDEM or following along at home, you may have seen [Andy Piper and I announce this](https://fosdem.org/2026/schedule/event/HJYRFF-tending-the-herd/) or [post about it](https://mastodon.social/@Mastodon/115989801184595302)!) This new sign-up method will replace the “join mastodon.social” button with the closest geographic server in the correct language based, on data surfaced by the app store (so it will only be on the Android and iOS apps, at least to start). The experiment will be designed to ensure that server admins remain in control: meaning they will have the power opt-in and -out as needed, or reduce the percentage of sign-ups flowing their way. If you want to be a part of that, let us know here.

Our ultimate goal is to reimagine Mastodon's onboarding process (you heard about that yesterday from Renaud and Imani). For now, we want to run some onboarding experiments to test our ideas and learn what can work for us and for everyone on the network. We also intend to improve server discovery by improving our Server Directory. This starts with moving the directory to a new pull-request-based system so admins can submit, update, or remove entries themselves. Another change we want to make is to add more and new ways to “classify” different types of servers including demographics, interests, and regions.

## We’re getting off Discord: Join us on Zulip

Finally, we're taking concrete steps to demonstrate our commitment to our values by moving as much of our digital infrastructure to free and open source software (FOSS) as possible. This is a long-term project. We’re going to move slowly, and we’re not committing to moving everything to FOSS. We’re taking our time, because we need to balance achieving our mission with evolving our operations.

So today, please join us in leaving Discord. Our community chat space is now on Zulip. If you’re a donor, you’ll receive instructions for joining a special space on Zulip where you’ll hear the latest from us before anyone, and help us shape the future of Mastodon too. (If you’re not already, you can [become a donor here](https://joinmastodon.org/sponsors#donate))
